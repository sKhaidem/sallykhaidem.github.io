Research Vision

From Capture to Understanding: Visual Sensing for Intelligent Perception

Event-Based Vision Computational Imaging Neural Rendering 3D Gaussian Splatting Vision Foundation Models

My research explores the complete visual information pipeline, from image capture and representation to reconstruction, neural rendering and scene understanding. By combining novel sensing modalities with learning-based computer vision, I develop computational methods for robust visual perception in challenging real-world environments.

Capture → Representation → Compression → Reconstruction → Rendering → Understanding

Research Themes

Problems I work on

Four connected research problems, spanning sensing to scene understanding.

Novel Visual Sensing

Conventional cameras struggle under motion blur, low light and high dynamic range. I work on event cameras and hybrid sensing modalities that capture visual information conventional sensors miss.

Computational Imaging

Raw sensor data is rarely usable directly. I design representations and reconstruction pipelines that recover high-quality visual signals from sparse, noisy or unconventional capture.

Neural Rendering

Turning captured and reconstructed data into renderable 3D scenes. I work with NeRF and 3D Gaussian Splatting to synthesize novel views, guided by event and multimodal cues.

Intelligent Visual Perception

The end goal of the pipeline is understanding. I study how vision foundation models and learned representations support robust, generalizable scene understanding.
