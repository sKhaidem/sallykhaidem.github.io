Sally Khaidem

I develop computational methods that transform visual information from capture to understanding by combining novel sensing modalities with computer vision to enable robust perception in challenging real-world environments.

PhD Candidate, Department of Electrical Engineering
Indian Institute of Technology Madras

Research Vision

From Capture to Understanding: Visual Sensing for Intelligent Perception

Event-Based Vision Computational Imaging Neural Rendering 3D Gaussian Splatting Vision Foundation Models

My research follows the visual information pipeline end to end — from capture and representation through compression and reconstruction, to rendering and understanding.

Capture → Representation → Compression → Reconstruction → Rendering → Understanding

Research Themes

I have worked on four connected research problems, spanning sensing to scene understanding.

Novel Visual Sensing

Capturing visual information conventional cameras miss — motion blur, low light, high dynamic range — through event cameras and hybrid sensing.

Event Cameras Hybrid Sensing HDR Capture

Computational Imaging

Recovering high-quality visual signals from sparse, noisy or unconventional capture through learned compression and reconstruction.

Compression Reconstruction Light-Field Imaging

Neural Rendering

Synthesizing novel views from captured data using NeRF and 3D Gaussian Splatting, guided by event and multimodal cues.

NeRF 3D Gaussian Splatting Novel View Synthesis

Intelligent Visual Perception

Turning reconstructed scenes into robust, generalizable understanding using vision foundation models.

Vision Foundation Models Scene Understanding Video Panoptic Segmentation
