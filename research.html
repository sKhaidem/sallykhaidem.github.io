Research Areas

My research follows the journey of visual information from capture to understanding. It explores how visual data is sensed, represented, compressed, reconstructed, rendered and ultimately interpreted by intelligent systems. Each theme below covers the problem, the publications it has produced, and the ongoing or completed projects.

Capture → Representation → Compression → Reconstruction → Rendering → Understanding

Novel Visual Sensing

Capture · Representation

Conventional frame-based cameras struggle under motion blur, low light and high dynamic range. I work on event cameras and hybrid sensing modalities that record visual information conventional sensors miss, and on representations that make this asynchronous data usable by standard vision pipelines.

Intelligent Visual Perception

Understanding

The end goal of the pipeline is understanding, not just reconstruction. I study how vision foundation models and learned representations support robust, generalizable scene understanding — including video panoptic segmentation and event-driven semantic understanding of dynamic scenes.
