Research

What I work on

My research follows the visual information pipeline end to end — from how a scene is captured, through how it is represented, compressed and reconstructed, to how it is rendered and ultimately understood. Each theme below sits at a different stage of that pipeline.

Capture → Representation → Compression → Reconstruction → Rendering → Understanding
Capture · Representation

Novel Visual Sensing

Conventional frame-based cameras struggle under motion blur, low light and high dynamic range. I work on event cameras and hybrid sensing modalities that record visual information conventional sensors miss, and on representations that make this asynchronous data usable by standard vision pipelines.

Representative work

Towards Efficient Neuromorphic Data Processing: A Novel Representation with Lossless Spatio-Temporal Compression VCIP 2025 Paper
Lightweight and High-Speed Contextual Intensity Image Reconstruction from Event-Based Sensors with Adaptive FastDynamicNet JEI 2025 Paper
High Resolution Multi-exposure Stereo Event-Intensity-Depth Database of Natural Scenes ICETA 2025 Paper
Compression · Reconstruction

Computational Imaging

Raw sensor data is rarely usable directly. I design compression schemes and reconstruction pipelines that recover high-quality visual signals from sparse, noisy or unconventional capture — including light-field data for auto-stereoscopic displays.

Representative work

A Deep Belief Network Approach to Scalable Compression of Light Field Data for Auto-Stereoscopic Displays BMVC 2024 Paper
An Integrated Representation & Compression Scheme Based on Convolutional Autoencoders with 4D-DCT Perceptual Encoding for High Dynamic Range Light Fields CICT 2024 Paper
Rendering

Neural Rendering

Turning captured and reconstructed data into renderable 3D scenes. I work with NeRF and 3D Gaussian Splatting for novel view synthesis, guided by event and multimodal cues to handle motion blur and high dynamic range that conventional capture cannot resolve.

Representative work

Blur to Brilliance: Neuromorphic Data Guided Deblurring and HDR Novel View Synthesis IEEE Access 2025 Paper
Understanding

Intelligent Visual Perception

The end goal of the pipeline is understanding, not just reconstruction. I study how vision foundation models and learned representations support robust, generalizable scene understanding — including video panoptic segmentation and event-driven semantic understanding of dynamic scenes.
